1. Project Overview
1.1 Introduction
Prepify is a web-based collaborative platform designed to help university students share, organize,  and rank handwritten solutions to academic problems and exam questions. The platform addresses  the common challenge students face when trying to access quality study materials, particularly  handwritten problem-solving notes that are often shared informally through messaging  applications.
1.2 Background Study
The background study reveals several key insights about the current landscape of academic  resource sharing:

Docsity is a global platform where students share lecture notes, solved papers, and study guides.  It features categorized libraries, ratings, and a reputation system for contributors. However, the  platform contains an overwhelming amount of material, and many resources are locked  behind premium access, making it difficult for students to quickly find specific handwritten  or course-focused notes.
Thinkswap allows students to exchange study materials using a credit-based system. It  organizes notes by subject and academic level, encouraging contribution through an upload-to download model. But its credit and purchase-based access model limits free availability, and  handwritten problem-solving notes are often scarce or difficult to navigate.
Ocenot is a Bangladeshi marketplace where students buy and sell academic notes, with search  options by course and university. It focuses mainly on paid, downloadable materials, and does  not offer community-driven ranking or any AI-based features. As a result, it is not optimized  for quickly finding free, handwritten problem-solving notes or generating summaries and quizzes  from uploaded content.
